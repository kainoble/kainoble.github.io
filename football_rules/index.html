1. The Field of Play

The field must be rectangular and marked with lines. It includes the goal area, penalty area, and corner arcs. The two longer boundary lines are touchlines, and the two shorter lines are goal lines.

2. The Ball

The ball must be spherical and made of leather or other suitable materials. It must have a circumference of 68-70 cm and weigh between 410-450 grams.

3. The Number of Players

A match is played by two teams, each consisting of no more than 11 players, one of whom is the goalkeeper. A match may not start if either team has fewer than seven players.

4. The Players' Equipment

Players must wear a jersey or shirt, shorts, socks, shin guards, and footwear. Goalkeepers must wear colors that distinguish them from other players and referees.

5. The Referee

The referee ensures the rules are followed and has full authority to enforce them in connection with the match.

6. The Assistant Referees

Two assistant referees help the referee in controlling the match. They indicate when the ball is out of play, offside offenses, and other duties as directed by the referee.

7. The Duration of the Match

A match consists of two 45-minute halves with a 15-minute half-time interval. The referee may add additional time for stoppages.

8. The Start and Restart of Play

A kick-off starts the match or restarts it after a goal is scored. A dropped ball restarts play when the referee stops the match for any other reason.

9. The Ball In and Out of Play

The ball is out of play when it completely crosses the goal line or touchline. It is in play at all other times, including when it rebounds from a goalpost, crossbar, or referee.

10. The Method of Scoring

A goal is scored when the entire ball passes over the goal line between the goalposts and under the crossbar, provided no offense has been committed by the scoring team.

11. Offside

A player is offside if they are nearer to the opponent's goal line than both the ball and the second last opponent unless they are in their own half of the field or level with the second last opponent.

12. Fouls and Misconduct

Fouls are penalized by awarding a free kick or penalty kick. Misconduct may result in a caution (yellow card) or sending-off (red card).

13. Free Kicks

Free kicks are either direct or indirect. A direct free kick can be scored directly against the opposing team, while an indirect free kick must touch another player before a goal can be scored.

14. The Penalty Kick

A penalty kick is awarded for fouls committed by the defending team within their penalty area. The ball is placed on the penalty mark, and only the goalkeeper can defend the kick.

15. The Throw-In

A throw-in is awarded when the whole ball crosses the touchline, and it is taken by a player of the team opposite to that of the player who last touched the ball.

16. The Goal Kick

A goal kick is awarded when the whole ball crosses the goal line, last touched by a player of the attacking team, and is taken from anywhere within the goal area by a player of the defending team.

17. The Corner Kick

A corner kick is awarded when the whole ball crosses the goal line, last touched by a player of the defending team, and is taken from within the corner arc nearest to where the ball crossed the goal line.
